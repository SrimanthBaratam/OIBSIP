Steve Jobs

1955 – 2011

“Design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Design is how it works.”

Biography

Steven Paul Jobs was an American entrepreneur, inventor, and business magnate. He was born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California, and was adopted shortly after birth. Steve Jobs is best known as the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Apple Inc., a company that transformed multiple industries including personal computing, mobile phones, music, and digital publishing.

In 1976, Steve Jobs co-founded Apple along with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. Together, they introduced the Apple I and Apple II computers, which helped popularize personal computing. Jobs’ vision was not just about technology, but about creating products that were simple, elegant, and user-friendly.

After leaving Apple in 1985, Jobs founded NeXT Inc., a computer platform development company, and later became the CEO of Pixar Animation Studios. Under his leadership, Pixar revolutionized animated films with classics such as *Toy Story*, *Finding Nemo*, and *The Incredibles*.

Steve Jobs returned to Apple in 1997, when the company was struggling. He led Apple’s remarkable comeback by launching iconic products such as the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad. These innovations changed the way people interact with technology and made Apple one of the most valuable companies in the world.

s Known for his perfectionism, creativity, and relentless pursuit of excellence, Steve Jobs believed in thinking differently. His passion for innovation and design continues to inspire entrepreneurs, developers, and designers across the globe.

Legacy

Steve Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011, but his legacy lives on through the products he created and the philosophy he championed. He proved that technology and art can coexist and that great ideas can truly change the world.
